buzz 生命周期
==========================================


本章中到目前为止,我们从宏观上已经探索了许多建设 `buzz` 的要点.
在进入一些具体的例子之前,需要学习的最后一块时髦的理论: `buzz` 生命周期.

每当你通过一套流程建立 `buzz` 时.
这一套流程程序,能确保你有效地计划,更好的达成你的预告目标,并从中总结出经验.
这些步骤有助于形成 `buzz` 决策的最佳实践,
能帮助你更好的规划和组织,如何激发你社区中的人们.


包含四个阶段:

计划 Planning:
    坐下来梳理你想要实现的什么?如何实现? 以及涉及的资源.
集结 Buildup:
    替代直接路线,为何不从前戏开始? 在进入主要任务前,先集结些兴带劲的事件. 
通告 Announce:
    这是 `buzz` 的核心,我们就是在这里完成推进的.
回顾 Review:
    复查我们所作的,评估什么作到了,什么没有做到,以及下次如何改进.



很多新人在 `buzz` 建设时常常直接进行公告,之于规划却一带而过.
我强烈反对这种行为.
`buzz` 是种艺术,
如果正确执行可以为您的社区带来令人难以置信的变化,
但同时,如果弄宁了就会造成持久的损害.
坚持规划和反馈将避免损伤.
为了解释为何每一个阶段是重要的,
我使用 `5-A-Day` 为例子,这也涉及在第4章阐述的 `buzz` 周期.



`5-A-Day` 是我设想的一个有关健康饮食的项目.
在许多国家,作为健康饮食的标志,它建议你每天应该吃5种水果和蔬菜.
这使健康饮食转换为一个容易记住的度量值,
从而令人们可以在他们的日常生活中运用起来.



在那段时期,我们非常清楚如何处理错误列表.
作为用户高速增长的项目,Ubuntu 社区没有bug总数. 
运用每天5种水果和蔬菜的鼓励技巧,
我们形成了 `5-A-Day` 的倡议: 鼓励社区成员每天处理5份 bug .
启动该项目取得了一些令人难以置信的进步.

作为例证,让我们来探查一下例子中 `buzz` 不同阶段的建设行为.


规划 Planning
------------------------------------------------------------------------------



`buzz` 需要规划的原因是,
你需要综合考虑你的目标/工具/资源,如何激励/推出你的计划.
你总想通过努力,尽可能多的从社区榨取更多的关注和注意.
总是期望获得最大的投资回报.


首先,这是坐下来,主动思考你的 `buzz` 中不同属性和元素的好时机.
你应该对下列问题有自己的答案:

- 你想达成什么?
- 你必须依靠哪些媒介来达成?
- 你开始前,必须完成哪些准备工作?
- 你应当邀请什么人加入`buzz`,他们各司何职?
- 整个 `buzz` 建设周期的时间表是怎样的?



这些问题的答案会给为你的目标达成给出坚实的思路.
当然,对于实现目标,
仅意识到这些问题的答案是不够的.
当然,如果你将和他人协同工作,
就应该在第一时间,将这些答案文档化.
并确保所有人都明白.



在`5-A-Day` 项目中,我同我的团队一齐工作,Daniel Holbach 和 Jorge Castro.
筹备工作包括准备一些技术设施和工具,一些文件,和一个时间表.
我们通过一些会议确立了计划,
商讨了各种条件的到位,
并指定了 `buzz` 各个阶段的最后期限.


.. topic:: "死线"保持你的活力 DEADLINES KEEP YOU ALIVE

    很多人痛恨最后期限("死线",deadlines).
    对于多数人,尤其是那些享受社区的自由形式的人们,"死线"是不受待见的.
    但是,必须不料推动他们来作事儿!
    "死线"是目标达成的关键.
    在本章中,我们的目标是有效的 `buzz`.
    当你的 `buzz` 建设工作涉及多人时,
    你需要确保每个人都能按时提供他们的贡献.
    确保在最后期限前,他们的文档已经存于某处.
    在我的团队中,我们将预先计划的最后期限标注在共用的日历中.
    这是一种可以提醒"死线"的有用的手段.
    关键是确保期限标注在一个谁都看得了的地方.
    如果标在一个文件里丢到笔记本电脑中,
    那对任何人都没用.



建设 Buildup
------------------------------------------------------------------------------




接下来,要作的事情就能令人兴奋了.
这个阶段,使我想起经常鼓吹的"有些事情最好留给想象力",这是真的.
在这个阶段,我们要作的特点就是--捉弄人.
我们要作的事情,必须激起他们的好奇心,吊足他们的胃口.
这个阶段完成时,你就能为宣布准备一些你在做什么的令人难忘的 `buzz` 了.

宣告 Announce
------------------------------------------------------------------------------


At this point in the cycle, there should be some rampant speculation regarding the hints you have been dropping in the buildup. You should be seeing suggestions from the sublime to the ridiculous. Don’t go too far with the buildup, though. Allow just a few weeks before you reveal your mystery with an announcement.

在这个阶段,会有一些猖獗的投机活动,不断冒出来提醒你已经不成了.
你应该看到从崇高到荒谬的各种建议.
当然,你别在 `buzz` 建设走得太远.
在你正式掲秘前,至多进行几周的建设.


When announcing you need to ensure you answer all of the most immediate questions the speculators have. If after all the buildup you don’t come through with a smörgåsbord of answers, it will simply cause frustration. You want those riddled with curiosity to be delighted to have their curiosity quenched when they hear the news.

在你进行宣告时,一定要确保包含所有投机者最关注的问题的直接回答.
否则,在期待这么久后,依然没有靠谱的答复,只能告成挫折.
你不期望那些积极的好奇宝宝在听到消息时,全体"淬火"吧?!

The first step when announcing is to point someone somewhere to read, hear, or watch your announcement. You should have a single location to direct people to. For most communities, this is a website. Your goal now is to make the page easy to read.

宣告的第一步,是准备好一个地方,以便所有人都能读/听/看到你的公告.
你应该有个固定的位置,引导人们.
对于大多数社区,这意味着是网站.
你现在的目标是令页面容易阅读.

Most announcements that communities tend to make are posted on their website, but there is an important consideration to bear in mind with web announcements: computer screens are hard to read. Jakob Nielsen, one of the world’s highest regarded usability gurus, wrote about the impact of screen text on readers (http://www.useit.com/alertbox/whyscanning.html )

往往大多数社区都在其网站上发布公布,
但请铭记这一重要因素: 计算机屏幕上的文字其实很难阅读.
Jakob Nielse ~ 算是世界上最牛可用性大师之一,
对于屏幕文字对读者的影响有专文论述（http://www.useit.com/alertbox/whyscanning.html）

::

    Reading from computer screens is tiring for the eyes and about 25 percent slower than reading from paper. No wonder people attempt to minimize the number of words they read. To the extent this reason explains users’ behavior, they should read more when we get high-resolution, high-scanrate monitors in five years since lab studies have shown such screens to have the same readability as paper.

    通过肉眼从电脑屏幕读是很累人的,
    比从纸上慢25％左右.
    对应的,人们就会下意识的试图尽量减少他们读的文字数量.
    为此也就能够一定程度上理解用户的行为,想让他们应该阅读更多就必须使用高分辨率屏幕,
    五年以来实验室的研究表明这种高分辨率屏幕有像纸一样的可读性.


With reading on screens known to be more tiring, this behavior naturally translates to people wanting to read less and scan more. As such, we need to deliver our announcement quickly and effectively.

在当前的各种屏幕上阅读,当然更加累了,
这种行为自然转化为人们希望阅读更少,并扫视更多.
因此,我们需要令公告可以快速并高效的得以传达!

It’s important that we get our announcement in perspective: it is going to be one of hundreds of things that the person will read on the Internet that day. We need to stand out. We need to grab the reader’s attention and deliver our content.
Nielsen’s solution to this problem is simple: write half as much. In his excellent “Writing for the Web” article (http://www.useit.com/alertbox/9703b.html ), Nielsen recommends three guidelines that can help:

重要的是我们必须从自身角度宣告:
这是当天数以百计的人将在互联网上阅读的事情之一.

我们必须站出来,抓住读者的注意力传达我们的内容.

Nielsen对这个问题的解决很简单:
写一半就够了.

在他的知名文章~ "网络写作"（http://www.useit.com/alertbox/9703b.html）中,
给出了很有帮助的三项准则::

- Be succinct : write no more than 50% of the text you would have used in a hardcopy publication.
- Write for scannability : don’t require users to read long, continuous blocks of text.
- Use hypertext to split up long information into multiple pages.

- 简洁: 不要超过你必须印刷出来的内容的50%
- 为扫视而写: 不需要用户阅读长连续文本块
- 使用超文本系统将长信息分割入多个页面.

We are trying to avoid swathes of text. We need to architect our announcement so our readers can skip over parts and get straight to the meat.

我们必须努力避免大片的文字.
我们需要精心构筑公告,
让读者可以跳过部分内容,依然可以获得核心内容.


Let’s look at an example. Imagine we are writing an announcement to solicit papers for a conference on renewable energy. Let’s start with a high suck factor and write an announcement we can tear apart afterward

让我们来看看一个例子.
想象一下,我们正在编写一个公告,征求可再生能源会议的论文.
让我们从一个最恶心的必然会丢弃的格式开始::

    Call for Papers Open

    Cranfield Green Alliance is a renewable energy conference that takes place in Cranfield, Bedfordshire. The conference is located at Cranfield University and runs from 10–12 November 2009. The conference covers a range of topics including renewable energy, alternative lifestyles, green cooking, ecological trends, and more. We are now opening up our call
    for papers and encourage a variety of environmental professionals to submit presentations
    in their area of expertise. Papers on a range of subjects are welcome and we would
    encourage you all to submit something soon. The conference attracts a wide range of
    attendees and exhibitions, and we welcome your involvement in this important event.
    Your contributions as a visitor or a speaker will be valuable. To submit your paper you
    should email papers@cranfieldgreenalliance.co.uk no later than 1st October 2009. We look forward to your submissions!

    征集论文公开赛

    Cranfield 大学绿色联盟是 Bedfordshire郡 Cranfield 当地的再生能源会议.此次会议位于Cranfield大学,时间从2009年11月10日至12日.会议涵盖范围包括可再生能源,另类的生活方式,绿色烹饪,生态趋势,等多个主题.现在开放会议呼吁文件,并鼓励各种环境的专业人员提交他们专业领域的简报.欢迎大家快速提交一系列课题论文.本次会议吸引了广泛的与会者和展览,我们欢迎您参与这一重要事件.您仅参加或是宣传依然是有价值的贡献.您应不迟于2009年10月1日向电子邮件papers@cranfieldgreenalliance.co.uk 提交文件.我们期待着您的提交！

Friends, what we have just experienced is unremarkable, flat, and about as exciting as a paint- drying competition. I am sorry I subjected you to that paragraph: I realize you will never get those minutes back. Consider it a sacrifice for your community.

好吧,刚刚看到的公告,是不值一提,呆板,有关油漆干燥竞赛的令人无法兴奋的.
对不起,我展示的这种行文:
我知道你永远不会再查阅这些文字.因为它们在牺牲你社区的形象.

OK, let’s apply some of the guidance we have discussed so far. Let’s make the language exciting and inspirational, break up the paragraph so it is easier to read and scan, and make it succinct and clear. Tighten your seat belts. Here we go

OK,让我们对通告的讨论再进一步.
让我们令行文令人鼓舞和兴奋起来,打破固有段落,
使其更容易阅读和扫视,并使其简洁明了.
拧紧你的安全带.走起::

    Cranfield Green Alliance Call For Papers Open!

    10–12 November 2009: Cranfield University, Cranfield, Bedfordshire, England. Leading the way to define a new future fueled by renewable energy.

    Including exciting and industry-relevant topics such as renewable energy, alternative lifestyles, green cooking, ecological trends, and more. Leaders of the field bring a great opportunity to learn from the finest minds in the industry.

    PLAY YOUR PART IN THE REVOLUTION

    Do you want to get your voice heard? Do you want to help inspire and encourage a new generation of renewable energy? We thought so. It’s time to submit a paper...

    Good luck!

    Cranfield 大学绿色联盟开放论文征集！

    2009年11月10日至12日:Cranfield大学,英国Bedfordshire大学,共同引领全新定义可再生能源燃料的未来.

    包括诸如可再生能源,另类的生活方式,绿色烹饪,生态趋势,更令人兴奋和行业相关的主题.
    带来向该領域最优秀的头脑学习的最好机会.

    融入这次革命

    想令你的声音得到聆听? 想共同推动和鼓励新一代可再生能源吗?
    是时候提交你的文件...

    提交任何有关绿色主题的论文,将立即面对超过500人的专业读者.

    如何提交:
    2009年! 10月1日前发送 papers@cranfieldgreenalliance.co.uk!

    好运!


In this example we performed a number of steps to brighten our announcement. This included:

- Separating out the key parts of the message into separate headings and paragraphs.
- Converting the language to be more rabble-rousing and inspiring.
- Engaging in a rhetorical dialog with the reader by asking questions and clearly showing that the answer was to submit a paper, which is the very aim of the announcement.

在这个例子中,我们进行了一系列处理来让公告醒目:

- 将关键信息分离成单独的标题和段落.
- 选择蛊惑人心,有鼓舞力的措辞.
- 通过设问,来明确要提交文件,这是通告的唯一目的.


Your announcement page should pass the elevator test: it should get the reader up to speed with what you are announcing within a minute.

您的公告页面,应该通过电梯测试:
它应该让任意读者在一分钟之内获得公告的必要信息.


Let’s get back to our 5-A-Day example. When we were constructing the 5-A-Day announcement, we produced a page that identified the primary concept of 5-A-Day, how people could get involved, and what they needed to do. Each of the different pieces of information had individual headings, and emphasis was used extensively. View the page at https://wiki.ubuntu.com/5-A-Day to see the principles in this section in action—and with a successful outcome.

让我们回到的 `5-A-Day` 的例子.
为了发布 `5-A-Day` 公布,我们制作了一个网页,
包含 `5-A-Day` 确定的基本概念,
人们可以如何参与, 他们需要做什么.
每个信息的不同部分有个别的标题,
重点是广泛使用.

查看 https://wiki.ubuntu.com/5-A-Day 页面,
对比前述原则,
就明白为什么行动得以成功了.


回顾 Review
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Postmortems are hugely valuable in any kind of work, and if you don’t perform them, you never learn how to improve. Whether you are evaluating how well you handled a discussion, cooked a meal, taught your kids how to play football, or built community buzz, a review can uncover great opportunities for improvement:

对于任何工作,事后回顾总是非常宝贵的,
如果你不这么作,将永远不会了解如何改善.
无论是评估如何改善讨论,煮熟一顿饭,教孩子如何踢足球,或社区 `buzz` 建设嗡嗡声,
回顾总是可以发现高效改进的机会:

Efficiency:
    When you review your work, it gives you an opportunity to identify areas that are inefficient and redundant. You can use these as a basis for improvement.

效率:
    每当回顾您的工作,会给你一个机会来识别哪些领域是低效和冗余的.这就是你改进的基础.

Feedback:
    Gathering feedback from the people who consumed your buzz is a great way to see what they felt worked and what didn’t. This is a great opportunity to get feedback on your writing, structure, and the other concepts throughout this chapter.

反馈:
    收集那些接触到 `buzz` 的人们的反应, 是最好的方式来明确什么建设活动有效,什么无效.
    这绝对是最恰当的机会,来对你的文字,组织,和其他贯穿本章的概念,完成针对性反馈.

Ideas:
    When any kind of postmortem of an approach occurs, it almost always generates new ideas. These will help future buzz cycles to be more effective.

思路:
    每当进行事后回顾时,几乎总是有新的想法产生.这将有助于未来更有效的发动 `buzz` 建设.

In the review phase, revisit the questions you asked in the planning phase and compare the plan with what happened:

在审查阶段,复读规划阶段的问题,对比计划自问究竟发生了什么事:

- Did you stick to your aim and communicate it well to others?
- Did you identify the right outcomes to achieve?
- Were your chosen mediums the most suitable?
- Did you prepare effectively?
- Did you communicate well to others involved in the buzz about what needed to be done?
- Was your timeline for the buzz cycle accurate? Did you hit your deadlines?


- 你坚持你的目标,并将其传达给别人呢？
- 你确定正确的结果达成了吗？
- 您所选择的媒介是否最合适？
- 您的准备有效吗？
- 你和加入 `buzz` 的人们有效沟通过嘛? 他们都明白自己要作什么嘛?
- 你制定的本次 `buzz` 周期时间精确吗? 有打破最后期限吗？

To make this process effective, you should gather feedback from members of your community. Seek to gather responses from those who will provide you with constructive advice of what worked and what didn’t. Remember, much of the goal here is to identify flaws in your approach. Flaws are nothing to be embarrassed about: they are opportunities to do even better next time.

为了使这个过程的有效,你应该收集社区成员的意见.
收集那些针对没有作到的建设性意见.
